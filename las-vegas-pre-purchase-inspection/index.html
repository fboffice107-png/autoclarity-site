How it works

From listing to clear answer

  1. Submit the vehicle and location. Tell us what you’re looking at and where it is — it takes a few minutes.
  2. AutoClarity reviews the request. Vehicle, location and seller access are checked personally, normally the same day.
  3. Receive your exact price and available times. No surprises — the full price is shown before you commit to anything.
  4. Select a time, accept the agreement, and pay securely. Payment through Stripe reserves your appointment.
  5. The technician inspects the vehicle. At the dealership or seller’s location, with a road test and computer scan where permitted and supported.
  6. Receive your written results. Findings, photos, repair priorities, and a clear Proceed / Negotiate / Do Not Proceed recommendation.

Submitting the form doesn’t guarantee an appointment: your request is confirmed once it’s been reviewed, the seller permits access, you’ve selected a time, and payment is complete.

    FAQ

    Common questions

    How long does an inspection take?

    Typically 60–120 minutes on site depending on the vehicle and access, plus time afterward to prepare your written results. Same-day digital findings are provided when applicable.

    Does the vehicle have to be at a dealership?

    No. AutoClarity is mobile and inspects at dealerships, private sellers’ locations, or anywhere in the service area with safe access and seller permission.

    Do I need the seller’s permission?

    Yes. The seller controls access to the vehicle. Confirming that they allow an independent inspection (most dealerships do) before booking avoids wasted trips.

    Is a road test guaranteed?

    No — a road test happens when the seller permits it, the vehicle appears safe to drive, and it’s lawful at that location. If it isn’t possible, your results say so clearly.

    Will the vehicle be lifted?

    Not always. Underbody access depends on the location, permission, ground conditions, clearance and equipment. A full lift inspection may require a partner facility and an additional charge.

    Will AutoClarity negotiate with the seller for me?

    No. You get independent findings and clear repair priorities you can use to negotiate yourself. Findings are never shared with the seller without your say-so.

    Does the inspection guarantee the vehicle?

    No. A PPI is a professional opinion about the vehicle’s observable condition at the time of inspection — not a warranty. Hidden or intermittent problems may not be detectable, but you’ll know far more than you would from a test drive alone.

    What if the vehicle sells before my appointment?

    You can transfer the inspection to a different vehicle once for free. Details are in the cancellation policy shown before you pay.

    What if the seller refuses access?

    If access is refused before travel begins, you can reschedule or transfer to another vehicle at no charge. If it happens after the technician is already en route, the mobile-service portion may be retained.

    When do I pay?

    Only after your request is reviewed and you’ve seen your exact quote, chosen a time, and accepted the service agreement. Payment is processed securely by Stripe and reserves your appointment.

    What areas do you serve?

    Las Vegas, North Las Vegas, Henderson, Boulder City and the surrounding Clark County area. Travel within 15 miles of central Las Vegas is included; beyond that a small mobile-service charge applies, and anything past 40 miles is quoted individually.

    What exactly do I receive?

    Written findings with photographs, immediate safety concerns, repair priorities with estimated cost ranges where appropriate, an overall 1–10 condition score, and a clear Proceed / Negotiate–Repair First / Do Not Proceed recommendation.

    Do I need the VIN to request an inspection?

    No — you can submit without it. The VIN is confirmed before the appointment is finalized so the inspection matches the exact vehicle.

    Is the service available outside Las Vegas?

    The in-person inspection service currently covers the Las Vegas area only. The AutoClarity iPhone app for understanding car symptoms is available everywhere.
